Introduction

CBS is standing up an operations-first human capital practice, and you're leading it. You built and ran it before bringing it to CBS. It sells to operations leaders, never HR.

Two revenue shapes carry it: interim management, the fast and direct engine, and human capital consulting, a slow-burn add-on to CBS's existing engagements. Vinyl's job is to name it, package it, and take it to market on two lanes. This document lays out the plan in our six steps: research, offer, assets, channels, sales enablement, and measurement. The first asset, the interim management one-pager, is included alongside it for your review.

01 Step One · Research

The market, the buyer, and where CBS can win.

The market and what competitors do

Human capital is now treated as a core value-creation lever in private equity, not an HR cost center. Demand for interim and fractional leadership is rising alongside it. The competitors sort into three camps, with a clear gap between them:

TBM — the direct analog

Brands it as "Leadership Solutions": strategic interim leaders, leadership development, human capital management, and talent management, positioned at the point of execution. This is your playbook. Learn from it, don't copy it — the name is TBM's brand.

Big strategy / PE firms

AlixPartners and Alvarez & Marsal. Premium, PE value-creation lens; consultants take interim roles for accountability. Broad, expensive, and they tend to own one narrow slice.

Marketplaces & search

BluWave and Cerius match a vetted interim executive fast; Spencer Stuart and Russell Reynolds place a CHRO or assess leadership. Both place a person; neither drives the change on the floor.

The white space for CBS

Almost nobody combines operations-first, plant-floor execution with interim leadership plus the talent systems, hands-on, at a mid-market price. That is exactly where you and CBS sit.

The buyer and how they buy

An operations leader feeling a profitability problem with a people cause underneath. Triggers: slipping results, high turnover, untracked absenteeism, post-acquisition "do we have the right people?", and the urgent leadership-transition call. They don't search for an "interim firm" — they go to a firm they trust and ask, "Do you have anyone who could do this?" The consulting side is roughly 80% add-on to a traditional engagement; interim is the reverse, roughly 80% proactive inbound.

Economics and where CBS stands

Interim is the engine and high-margin: contract leaders, not on benefits, no cost when idle. Engagements run 3–12 months and are discounted from the traditional fee structure, so CBS can win $50–100M companies, not just conglomerates. CBS's online content is thin today, and CBS already sells a "Leadership and Change Management" service that overlaps this practice.

02 Step Two · Offer

What we sell, and what we call it.

Three capabilities plus the engine:

  • Interim management (the engine). An experienced contract leader runs a function and drives a 3–12 month transition.
  • Human capital consulting. The diagnostic: turnover and absenteeism data, a change-readiness assessment mapping resistors against key stakeholders, and a stakeholder analysis for the executive team.
  • Talent management. The systems: new talent-acquisition channels, retention and succession strategies, training and onboarding, redesigned org charts.
  • Change management. Woven through everything, not sold as its own line.

Messaging

The core line, in plain terms: the results a plant can't explain usually have a people cause underneath, and CBS fixes it as an operations problem.

  • Interim lane: when a critical seat is open or a transition is stalling results, CBS puts a proven operator in it, fast, to hold performance and drive the change.
  • Consulting lane: CBS finds the turnover, absenteeism, and leadership gaps behind slipping results, then builds the talent and leadership systems that make the gains hold.
  • Why it lands: operations-first and hands-on, spoken in the numbers operations leaders already own.

Language: use and avoid

  • Use: interim management, interim leadership; operations and results terms (throughput, margin, on-time delivery).
  • Avoid: fractional, embedded / embedded leadership, organizational effectiveness, and HR framing.

Naming principle: ontological and findable. Name it for what it is, no acronym, don't bury it, make it searchable. Learn from TBM's three mistakes: a separate LLC, an acronym ("TLS") that Googles to nothing, and dumbing it down out of cannibalization fear.

The three names we're proposing
01
Interim Management & Leadership
Front-loads the exact term buyers search. Strongest for findability.
02
Leadership & Talent Solutions
Broadest of the three: covers interim and the consulting side under one roof.
03
Operations Leadership & Talent
Anchors on "operations," the buyer's own language, not HR's.

All three stay plain and findable, and steer clear of TBM's exact "Leadership Solutions" brand. You react, we lock one.

  • Whichever wins, the flagship page stays titled Interim Management — the literal term buyers search.
  • Fold in the existing "Leadership and Change Management" page rather than run two competing "Leadership" pages.

04 Step Four · Channels

Two lanes to market.

Lane A · Internal

Enable the consultants

The 80%-add-on engine. CBS consultants are already inside active engagements; they surface the people problem and bring you in. A lead channel that runs on internal enablement, not ads.

Lane B · External

Own the trigger moment

For interim. Brand and awareness to existing clients and A&D / PE executives, not lead-gen search, because nobody Googles for it. Be the default call when a seat empties: website presence, real SEO/AEO, a LinkedIn launch, and a few high-end ads. Draft off your track record. Keep it "when your people leave, we're who to call," never "buy now."

05 Step Five · Sales Enablement

The consultants are the sales force.

Most of the consulting revenue comes through active engagements, so the CBS consultants are the real sales force. The biggest blocker you named: consultants won't mention a service if they fear a question they can't answer.

  • The cheat sheet. One page: the buying signals, operations-and-profitability framing (not HR), a few qualifying questions, and the unlock line — "I don't have all the answers; let me share what I've got and pull in Shannon" — plus who to route to.
  • The incentive. Consultants won't bring you in without a profit motive. Give them a piece and they will. A comp decision for CBS.
  • You on center stage. A recurring internal slot where you teach the team what you do and how to spot it, plus the weekly story-capture cadence.
  • Adoption goal. Beat TBM's two-year curve: smaller team, flexible leadership, no cannibalization fear.
06 Step Six · Measure

How we'll know it's working.

Leading indicators

Consultants surfacing the practice on live engagements, intros made, add-on scope signed, interim inbound calls, pages live and cleanly indexed, keyword findability, content and case studies published.

Lagging indicators

Revenue by bucket against the ~80% interim / 20% consulting shape, and adoption speed against the two-year TBM benchmark.

Conclusion

The fastest path to momentum: ship the quick wins first — finish the interim one-pager and build the consultant cheat sheet, neither of which waits on the name — then lock the name together and build out the website depth and case studies behind it. Run both lanes in parallel: enable the consultants inside active work, and keep interim awareness in front of A&D and PE leaders so CBS is the call when a seat empties. The target is to beat the two-year adoption curve you lived through at TBM.
